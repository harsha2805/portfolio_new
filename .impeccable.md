## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience — recruiters scanning for talent, potential freelance clients, and fellow developers. They arrive with no prior knowledge of HoloStack; the portfolio is the first and only impression. Browsed on desktop predominantly (creative/tech audience), but mobile matters.

**Job to be done**: Visitors need to quickly decide "is this person worth reaching out to?" The answer should be yes — and the interface itself should be evidence of the answer.

**Primary emotional takeaway**: *"This person is creative."* Not just that they can code, but that they have taste, originality, and make things that feel alive.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Innovative · Animated · Immersive

**Name context**: HoloStack — implies layering, depth, something that exists in multiple planes at once. Not flat. Not static.

**Copy tone**: Playful and witty. Self-aware without being try-hard. Confident without being arrogant. The kind of voice that writes "motion that earns its frame rate" and means it.

**Anti-personality**: Corporate, safe, templated. No hero section with a gradient button. No "I am a full-stack developer passionate about solving problems."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Both light and dark mode. Light is the default (paper `#f5f3ee`). Dark should feel like a different material, not just inverted.

**Visual tone**: Swiss editorial as the structural backbone — 12-col hairline grid, generous whitespace, precise typography. Motion and depth are what make it *HoloStack* specifically. The grid holds everything; animation happens within it and occasionally breaks through it for emphasis.

**Current palette**:
- Paper: `#f5f3ee` | Ink: `#0a0a0a`
- Accent 1 (purple): `#6c2ff2`
- Accent 2 (green): `#22c55e`
- Rule: `rgba(10,10,10,0.14)` | Ghost: `rgba(10,10,10,0.38)`

**Current fonts** (in use, review on next redesign pass):
- Inter Tight — body/UI sans
- JetBrains Mono — meta/labels
- Fraunces italic — editorial accent *(reflex pick — consider replacing with a less ubiquitous editorial serif on the next font pass)*

**References**: None specified. When sourcing inspiration, look at: technically ambitious portfolios with strong motion craft (Bruno Simon, Lusion, Active Theory energy) married to Swiss typographic rigour. The "immersive" quality should come from depth and motion, not from dark backgrounds and neon glows.

**Anti-references**: Generic dev portfolio templates. Glassmorphism. Purple-to-blue gradient hero sections. Anything that looks like it was generated from a prompt in 30 seconds.

### Design Principles

1. **Motion earns its frame rate.** Every animation communicates something — state, hierarchy, personality. If removing it loses nothing, it shouldn't be there.

2. **Structure first, then drama.** The Swiss grid is the skeleton. Surprise and delight happen *within* a precise system, not instead of one.

3. **Depth over decoration.** Effects should suggest layers and dimension (parallax, reveal, stagger) rather than sitting on the surface as cosmetic noise.

4. **Wit without noise.** Copy is playful; the interface doesn't shout. Restraint in decoration makes the moments of personality land harder.

5. **The portfolio IS the work.** The site itself must demonstrate the same craft as the apps it showcases. Mediocre presentation undercuts everything else.

### Technical Constraints
- React SPA (Vite 7, react-router-dom v6)
- Laravel 12 backend, catch-all route serves the SPA
- Inline styles (no CSS framework active in JSX components)
- CSS animations via `<style>` tags injected in JSX
- Target: modern browsers, no IE
- Accessibility: no specific WCAG level required, but semantic HTML and keyboard nav are good defaults
